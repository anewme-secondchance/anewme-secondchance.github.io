Expungement May Apply If:

  • Case dismissed
  • Found Not Guilty
  • No-billed by grand jury
  • Arrested but never charged

Steps to Expunge Your Record in Texas

  1. Confirm you qualify for expunction.
  2. Find your case details (county, court, case number).
  3. Complete a Petition for Expunction form.
  4. File the petition with the district court in the county where the arrest occurred.
  5. Pay the filing fee or request a fee waiver.
  6. Attend a court hearing if required.
  7. If approved, the court orders agencies to destroy the record.

Non-Disclosure May Apply If:

  • Completed deferred adjudication
  • Completed probation (certain offenses)
  • No new convictions during waiting period

⚠ Usually NOT Eligible If:

  • Family violence conviction
  • Sex offense conviction
  • Aggravated violent felony
  • Multiple repeat convictions

📜 Know Your Rights

  • You have the right to remain silent if questioned by law enforcement.
  • You have the right to request an attorney before answering questions.
  • You have the right to refuse consent to a search unless officers have a warrant.
  • You have the right to ask if you are free to leave during a stop.
  • You have the right to receive a copy of any paperwork you sign.
  • If you are on probation/parole, you must follow supervision terms — but you still maintain constitutional rights.

Important: Stay calm, do not argue on the scene, and document details afterward.

🗳 Voting Rights Restoration (Texas)

You CANNOT vote if:

  • You are currently incarcerated
  • You are on parole
  • You are on probation

You CAN vote once:

  • Your full sentence is completed
  • Parole is completed
  • Probation is completed

Voting rights are automatically restored in Texas after full completion of sentence — no special court process required.

Next Step:

  • Register to vote again if needed
  • Confirm your voter registration status
